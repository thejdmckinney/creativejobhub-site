Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about job detail tracking and documentation

How many photos can I upload per job and are there storage limits?

Unlimited photos per job with no storage caps included at $89/month. Upload as many before, during, and after photos as needed—whether that's 5 photos for a simple repair or 50+ photos for a complex installation. All photos are stored in full resolution with automatic cloud backup, accessible forever from any device. This is dramatically different from competitors who limit photos to 10-20 per job or charge storage fees after 1-5GB. The unlimited model means you can document thoroughly without worrying about hitting caps or surprise storage charges.

What does AI photo analysis actually do?

The AI automatically analyzes uploaded photos for quality issues (blurry, too dark, improper framing), identifies objects and problems in the image (damaged equipment, leaks, electrical issues), suggests relevant tags and categories, and flags photos that might need retakes. For example, if you upload a dark photo of an HVAC unit, the AI warns "Image may be too dark for documentation—consider retaking with better lighting." This helps ensure you capture usable photos in the field rather than discovering poor quality back at the office. The analysis runs in real-time as photos are uploaded, taking 2-3 seconds per image.

What job status options are available for tracking?

Six status levels track jobs from creation to completion: Scheduled (job booked but not started), En Route (technician traveling to location), In Progress (actively working on-site), Completed (work finished, awaiting invoice), Invoiced (invoice sent to customer), and Paid (fully closed). Each status change is timestamped and logged in the job history, creating a complete audit trail. You can also add custom sub-statuses for your specific workflow (e.g., "Waiting for Parts," "Customer Approval Needed"). The visual color-coding makes it easy to see job status at a glance across your entire operation.

How does the job history and audit log work?

Every action on a job is automatically logged with timestamp and user attribution: who created the job, when it was scheduled, every status change, notes added, photos uploaded, estimates linked, invoices generated, and payments received. The complete history is viewable in chronological order on the job record, creating a permanent audit trail. This is invaluable for resolving customer disputes ("When exactly did you do the work?" "See here—marked complete at 2:47pm on June 15th"), training new staff on best practices, and identifying process bottlenecks. Nothing can be deleted from the history—it's a permanent record.

Can I track expenses and receipts per job?

Yes—expense tracking with receipt photo documentation is built-in. Add line items for materials purchased, subcontractor costs, permit fees, or any job-specific expenses. Snap photos of receipts directly from your phone and attach them to expense entries for verification and tax documentation. The system automatically calculates total job costs vs revenue to show actual profit margin per job, helping you identify which types of work are most profitable. At tax time, export all expenses with receipt photos organized by job for easy accounting. Many customers recover $3,000-$8,000 annually in previously forgotten expense deductions.

How does photo documentation reduce customer disputes by 95%?

Comprehensive photo documentation eliminates "he said, she said" situations. When customers question what work was done, you can instantly show timestamped before/during/after photos proving the scope of work. When warranty claims arise, photos show the original condition and installation quality. When customers claim "you damaged something," before photos prove it was pre-existing. The 95% reduction comes from having irrefutable visual evidence for every job—most disputes dissolve immediately when you pull up photos. The few remaining disputes are typically legitimate issues you can resolve quickly because photos help diagnose what actually happened.
