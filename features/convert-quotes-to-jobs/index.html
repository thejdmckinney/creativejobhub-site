Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about estimate-to-job conversion

How does the one-click conversion actually work?

When a customer approves an estimate through the portal, you see a "Convert to Job" button. One click triggers database triggers that automatically copy all line items, pricing, customer details, service descriptions, photos, notes, and special instructions to a new job record. The system generates a job number, links back to the original estimate for reference, and optionally sends a confirmation email to the customer. Everything that was in the estimate—including labor, materials, deposits, discounts—transfers perfectly with zero manual re-entry. The entire process takes 2-3 seconds.

What happens if I need to modify the job after conversion?

Jobs remain fully editable after conversion. You can add line items, adjust pricing, change dates, assign different technicians, or modify service details—just like any other job. The system maintains a link to the original estimate so you can always reference what was approved, but the job operates independently. If you need to make significant changes that affect pricing, you can generate a change order that documents the modifications and gets additional customer approval before proceeding with the altered scope.

How much time does this actually save compared to manual entry?

Manual estimate-to-job transfer typically takes 10-15 minutes per job: copying line items, re-entering pricing, typing customer details, transferring notes, and scheduling. With one-click conversion, this becomes a 2-3 second process. For a business converting 120-200 estimates per year, that's 25-40 hours saved annually—equivalent to a full work week. The time savings compound because you also eliminate the 10-15 minutes typically spent fixing transcription errors when manually entered data doesn't match the original estimate.

Does conversion work with partial estimate approval?

Yes—if a customer wants to approve only certain line items from an estimate (e.g., "do the HVAC repair but skip the duct cleaning"), you can selectively convert specific line items to the job. Simply check the items to include before clicking convert, and only those services transfer. The system calculates the new total automatically, and you can send a confirmation showing exactly what was approved and what was excluded. This flexibility is crucial for field service businesses where customers often pick and choose from a menu of recommended services.

What happens to the estimate after it converts to a job?

The original estimate remains in the system for record-keeping and reporting, marked as "Converted" with a link to the resulting job number. This bidirectional linking means you can always trace a job back to its original estimate to see what was quoted versus what was actually done. The estimate stays searchable and accessible, which is valuable for warranty claims, customer disputes, or auditing your approval rates and pricing accuracy over time. Nothing gets deleted—you maintain a complete audit trail from quote to completion.

Is there a limit to how many estimates I can convert?

No—unlimited estimate-to-job conversions are included at $89/month with no per-conversion fees or volume restrictions. This is dramatically different from competitors who charge per-conversion fees ($2-5 each) or limit conversions on lower-tier plans. Whether you convert 10 estimates per month or 500, the cost stays the same. The unlimited model eliminates the perverse incentive to avoid using the feature due to cost concerns, and ensures you can convert every single approved estimate without hesitation.
