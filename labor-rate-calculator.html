üî• Pro Tips for Raising Your Rates

1. Stop Competing on Price

If you're the cheapest, you attract the worst clients. Premium pricing attracts serious customers who value quality and won't nickel-and-dime you.

2. Package Your Services

Instead of charging by the hour, create service packages. "Basic Maintenance: $299" sounds better than "$85/hour and it'll probably take 3-4 hours."

3. Specialize in Something

General handymen charge $65/hour. Kitchen remodeling specialists charge $125/hour. Same work, better positioning.

4. Target Affluent Areas

A homeowner in a upperclass neighborhood doesn't blink at $150/hour. They care about quality, reliability, and not wasting their time.

5. Look the Part

Clean truck, professional logo, branded shirts, and organized tools signal "premium" before you even start talking price.

6. Offer Maintenance Plans

Monthly recurring revenue is king. "Premium Plan: $499/month" is better than hunting for new customers every week.
