Frequently Asked Questions

What are the three stages of creosote buildup?

Stage 1 creosote is flaky or sooty, easily removed with a chimney brush during routine cleaning. Stage 2 is shiny, tar-like, and harder to remove, requiring more aggressive cleaning methods. Stage 3 is thick, hardened, highly combustible glaze that often requires professional removal or chemical treatment. Our software tracks which stage each chimney is at, with photos and dates, helping you schedule the right level of cleaning and document safety for CSIA compliance.

How does creosote tracking improve chimney safety?

Tracking creosote levels over time helps identify chimneys progressing from Stage 1 to Stage 2 or 3, allowing you to schedule cleaning before dangerous buildup occurs. The software alerts you when a chimney hasn't been serviced in 12+ months or shows increasing creosote levels between visits. This prevents chimney fires, protects homeowners, and provides documented proof of your professional recommendations for liability protection.

Can I take photos of creosote buildup?

Yes, you can capture photos of creosote buildup directly from your mobile phone during inspections. Photos attach to the specific chimney's record with the date, creosote stage, and your notes. This visual documentation is invaluable for showing customers why cleaning is needed, justifying pricing for Stage 3 removal, and providing evidence for insurance claims or liability cases.

How does historical creosote tracking work?

Every time you log creosote levels for a chimney, the software maintains a complete history showing dates, stages, photos, and cleaning performed. You can view trend charts showing if a chimney is getting worse over time, which wood types or burning patterns cause more buildup, and which chimneys need more frequent service. This helps educate customers and optimize your cleaning schedules.

Does creosote tracking integrate with CSIA inspections?

Absolutely. Creosote level logging is built directly into CSIA Level 1, 2, and 3 inspection checklists. When you complete an inspection, you log the creosote stage as part of the standard workflow. This data then appears in the generated CSIA inspection report PDF sent to customers, providing complete NFPA 211 compliance documentation for insurance and safety records.

Can I set automatic alerts for dangerous creosote levels?

Yes, the software can automatically alert you when a chimney reaches Stage 2 or Stage 3 creosote levels, hasn't been cleaned in 12+ months, or shows worsening trends between visits. You can also set custom alerts based on your professional judgment. These alerts help you proactively schedule cleaning appointments before dangerous situations develop, demonstrating your commitment to customer safety.
